Al2O3Ne

Al: aluminum
O: oxygen
Ne: neon

Under fire, aluminum powder reacts with oxygen in the air to form aluminum oxide. Neon, a stable element that doesn’t react, does have an effect. Its presence in the atmosphere affects oxygen flow and changes the way the aluminum oxide layer forms.

Al2O3Ne was constructed in a lab at the University of Chicago in order to study its physical tendencies and how it reacts in space. The experiment was conducted in collaboration with Andrzej Latka, a Ph.D. student in U of C's Nagel Group.
